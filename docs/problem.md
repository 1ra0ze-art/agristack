# Problem Statement — AgriStack Irish Potato Marketplace

## Street Problem

Farmers and cooperatives in Musanze District, Northern Province, Rwanda face significant income losses due to price manipulation and lack of direct access to bulk buyers.

## Field Research Quote

> "We harvest hundreds of kilograms of Irish potatoes, but middlemen come and offer us prices we cannot refuse because we have no other options. By the time our potatoes reach Kigali, the price has tripled — and we see none of that profit."  
> — *Farmer cooperative leader, Musanze, 2024*

## Root Causes

1. **Information asymmetry** — Farmers do not know current market prices for Irish potatoes.
2. **Middlemen exploitation** — Brokers buy low and sell high, capturing value that should reach farmers.
3. **No direct buyer access** — Cooperatives lack a platform to connect with aggregators, supermarkets, or exporters.
4. **No workflow transparency** — Bookings and harvest status are tracked informally, leading to disputes.
5. **Geographic isolation** — Musanze's terrain limits physical market access.

## Impact

- Average farmer earns 30–40% below fair market price.
- Post-harvest losses reach 20% due to delayed transactions.
- Cooperatives cannot plan supply chains without confirmed bulk orders.

## Proposed Solution

**AgriStack** — a web-based cooperative listing and bulk order management system that connects farmers/cooperatives directly to aggregators and buyers, with an admin layer ensuring quality control and transparency.
